About The Life Reconnected

The Life Reconnected offers scholars and enthusiasts of the early-twentieth century a glimpse of the textual culture of the era. Taking the American author Edith Wharton's heavily referential 1908 journal The Life Apart as its starting point, the project constructs a database of TEI-encoded documents that are hyperlinked with one another. These documents can be accessed via the menus labeled "Diary Entries," "Personography," "Placeography," and "Resography" above.

What is TEI? What is TEI Boilerplate?

As mentioned above, The Life Reconnected consists of TEI (Text Encoding Initiative) files. TEI is a set of guidelines for encoding humanities texts in a machine-readable form. Read more about TEI here.


The Life Reconnected uses John Walsh et al.'s TEI Boilerplate to digitally publish its TEI files on the Web. Read more about TEI Boilerplate here.

What's Next?

The Life Reconnected seeks to eventually evolve into a larger database that contains the entirety of Wharton's heretofore unpublished diary material, thus providing a more comprehensive account of early-twentieth century textual culture.
